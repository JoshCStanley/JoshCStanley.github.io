hotspot pheasant tail

Effective in rivers all season. It can be fished under an indicator or with a euro nymphing setup. A bright orange hotspot provides a trigger and point of difference from natural nymphs. This fly is also for sale in our shop.

Materials



Hook: #16 jig hook

Bead: 3mm tungsten slotted

Thread: Brown 8/0

Tail: Coq de Leon

Body: Pheasant tail

Rib: Thin copper wire

Collar: Orange thread


step 1



Place the bead on the hook, ensuring that the large slot faces upwards.

 

 

 

 

 

 

step 2



Dress the hook to about the halfway point.

 

 

 

 

 

 

step 3



At the halfway point tie in about 3 pieces of Coq de Leon tail and wrap back towards the bead.



step 4



Starting at the bead, tie in the copper wire and wrap both the wire and tail down to the bend of the hook. Make 1 wrap under the tail and wire to help kick it up.

step 5



Tie in 2 pieces of pheasant tail from the tip. Wrap back to the bead.




step 6



Wrap the pheasant tail forward to the bead and secure it with a few wraps of thread. I like to use hackle pliers for this.


step 7



wrap wire the opposite way to the pheasant tail and secure with a few wraps of thread. Tie a few half hitches with the thread then cut it off.

step 8



Change to orange thread and make 5-10 wraps then either whip finish or tie a half hitch and use super glue or head cement.

Squirmy worm

This fly works best early or late in the season and is particularly effective in coloured or secondhand water. This fly is also for sale in our shop.

materials



Hook: #14 wet fly hook

Bead: 3.3mm tungsten

Thread: Pink

Body/Tail: Squirmy worm material

step 1



Place the bead on the hook. I would normally use a silver or pink bead but I ran out so this one is tied with a copper bead.

 

step 2



Dress the hook. Wrapping from the bead to the bend of the hook.

 



step 3



Tie the squirmy worm material to one side of the hook.

step 4



Wrap the thread back to the bead


step 5



Wrap the material forward and secure it at the bead with a few wraps of thread.

step 6



Cut off any excess material and either whip finish or tie a few half hitches. Do not use any kind of super glue or head cement as it will destroy the squirmy worm material.


Final result



Your finished fly should look like this.

I always leave the tail long. You can always cut it short later but you can't cut it longer.

 

F-Fly

A small CDC dry fly that represents mayfly, caddis and other small insects. It is good for picky fish that might have refused other flies. This fly is also for sale in our shop.

materials



Hook: #18 dry fly

Thread: Brown

Body: Natural hares ear

Wing: Natural CDC

step 1



Dress the hook from the eye to the bend.

 




step 2



Create a dubbing noodle out of hares ear then wrap forwards stopping about 2mm from the eye of the hook.

 


step 3



Place 2-3 CDC feathers on top of each other and tie them on. The wing should be roughly the length of the hook.


step 4



Cut the ends of the CDC off and cover the loose ends with a few wraps of thread.


step 5



Dub a small amount of hares ear in front of the wing then tie off and cut the thread. Use a small amount of head cement to secure the thread.
